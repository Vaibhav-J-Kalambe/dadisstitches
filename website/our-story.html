How It All Began

A chai, a conversation,
and a calling.

In 2024, our founders met Savitri Dadi — a 67-year-old embroidery artist from Mumbai who had sewn thousands of designs in her lifetime but earned almost nothing from her craft. She had no platform. No market. Only her skill, and 55 years of patient practice.

That meeting changed everything. We realised that India's grandmothers carry a form of art that is irreplaceable — and they deserve fair wages, dignity, and a global audience.

Dadi's Stitches was our answer.

What We Stand For

Three promises we
will never break

Fair Wages, Always

₹400 guaranteed per shirt — before we take a single rupee of profit. Our Dadis are artists. They are paid like artists.

Zero Shortcuts

Every design is stitched by hand. A minimum of 4 hours per shirt. No machines. No shortcuts. No exceptions.

Dignity, Not Charity

Our Dadis are not charity cases. They are skilled professionals whose craft is their pride. We simply give it a market.
